Biography

Yi Cheng received his B.S. degree from Fudan University and his Ph.D. degree from Peking University, where he conducted doctoral research under the supervision of Prof. Zhongfan Liu. He subsequently carried out postdoctoral research at Rice University, working with Prof. James M. Tour.

His research focuses on the electrothermal upcycling of solid wastes and its applications in functional materials synthesis and environmental engineering. To date, he has published over 50 peer-reviewed journal articles, including more than 20 papers as first or corresponding author in leading journals such as Nature Sustainability, Nature Water, Nature Chemical Engineering, Nature Communications, and Journal of the American Chemical Society. He is also an inventor on more than 30 granted or filed patents in China and the United States, with several technologies currently advancing toward industrial implementation. He currently serves as a Youth Editorial Board Member for journals including Advanced Fiber Materials, Carbon Energy, Exploration, Carbon Neutralization, EcoEnergy, and SusMat.
