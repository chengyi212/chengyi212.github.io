Research

Our research group operates at the intersection of materials science, chemistry, and environmental engineering, with a particular focus on Flash Joule Heating (FJH) and related extreme-condition electrothermal synthesis technologies. We investigate new transformation mechanisms under extreme, transient electrothermal conditions, aiming to develop efficient, sustainable, and scalable strategies for solid waste valorization and functional material synthesis, contributing to resource security and environmental sustainability.

1. High-value conversion of solid wastes and resource circularity

We explore electrothermal-driven rapid and non-equilibrium conversion pathways to upgrade solid wastes into high-value functional materials and to recover critical elements. Representative studies include the carbon-thermal upcycling of waste plastics and composites (Nat. Sustain., 2024, 7, 452–462; Nat. Chem. Eng., 2025, 2, 405–406), as well as electrothermal approaches for battery recycling and critical-material recovery (Energy Environ. Sci., 2025, 18, 608–6093; Nat. Commun., 2024, 15, 6250).

2. Electrothermal-enabled environmental remediation and pollutant valorization

Our work advances electrothermal strategies that integrate pollutant destruction with resource recovery. This includes rapid degradation and mineralization of contaminants in soils and solid matrices (Nat. Commun., 2023, 14, 6371; Nat. Commun., 2024, 15, 6117), as well as closed-loop recovery of fluorine resources from fluorine-containing pollutants (Nat. Water, 2026, in press), establishing new paradigms that couple environmental remediation with circular resource utilization.

Electrothermal remediation and pollutant-to-resource valorization pathways.

3. Rapid materials synthesis under extreme conditions and environmental applications

We develop rapid and controllable synthesis routes for functional and nanostructured materials, and explore their applications in environmental catalysis, energy conversion, and resource cycling. Representative contributions include controllable synthesis of graphene and nanocarbon materials (J. Am. Chem. Soc., 2022, 144, 15562–15568; Adv. Funct. Mater., 2022, 32, 210349; ACS Nano, 2020, 14, 5938–5945; ACS Nano, 2025, 19, 38942–38953), rapid fabrication of one-dimensional nanowires and nanotubes (Matter, 2026, in press; ACS Nano, 2025, 19, 24904–24911), and the construction of two-dimensional heterostructures (J. Am. Chem. Soc., 2025, 147, 33735–33742).
